Introduction

Why is it important to study maths?

The National Extension College wrote following:
Maths is one of the foremost subjects for developing problem-solving skills and encouraging analytical thinking. These skills are valuable in a wide range of everyday scenarios. From calculating expenses, analysing data, and making strategic decisions, a solid foundation in maths empowers individuals to tackle challenges effectively.

Who am I?

I’m currently a student and doing an apprenticeship as an IT specialist in system integration. I completed basic German high school education (Gymnasium) but without the advanced level (Abitur). I really struggled with high school maths, and even with a lot of learning, it was hard to keep up with the other students.
I leaned the wrong way.

So why do you want to teach me how to learn math?

I’m not here to teach math.

My goal is to share my journey of getting better at math using mostly free resources and a bit of spare time. And I’m not just talking about high school math—I’m aiming for the level of a university math student.

Along the way, I’ve found plenty of resources created by brilliant mathematicians. But here’s the thing: they don’t always account for the struggles beginners face. That’s why I want to share my experiences—the wins, the setbacks, and the lessons—so you can learn from them and maybe avoid some of the same mistakes.

How much time do I need?

As much time as you’re willing to invest. The more time you invest, the faster you’ll learn. But even over a longer period, you can and will improve your maths and problem-solving skills.

Free Resources

There are really a lot of resources.

List of free Roadmaps and Courses

1. OSSU Curriculum Mathematics

A complete, degree-equivalent education in mathematics, crafted entirely from online materials. This structured curriculum is designed to meet the standards of an undergraduate math major, providing a clear learning path that typically spans two years. It's perfect for dedicated learners seeking a thorough and systematic foundation. It's mostly based on curses.

2. Mathematics Roadmap

Dive into mathematics through a carefully selected collection of books and exercises. This roadmap is more than a list; it's guided by a learning philosophy that emphasizes deep understanding, metacognition, and problem-solving. It’s an excellent choice for those who learn best by reading and engaging deeply with fundamental texts.

3. Self Study Map Mathematics

A thoughtfully curated collection designed to make mathematics accessible and engaging. This resource prioritizes pedagogical effectiveness and learner motivation over traditional, often overwhelming, textbooks. It's a shorter, high-quality starting point that helps you build momentum and enjoy the learning process.

4. MIT Open Courseware

MIT offers many of its mathematics courses online for free, including lecture notes, assignments, and exams. These resources are academically rigorous and ideal if you want to build a strong foundation or reach university-level proficiency in math.

5. KI Roadmap

AI topics. However, the quality can be inconsistent, and the content is often very shallow. These resources are best suited for very specific needs—not recommended for general learning or beginners.
As an example: ChatGPT Deep Research or Roadmap.sh.
